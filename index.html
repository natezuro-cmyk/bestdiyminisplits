      § 02

      The case for ditching your old system

      Six reasons · one technology
      01

      30–40% lower energy bills

      Modern inverter mini splits hit SEER2 20+ — drastically more efficient than window units, portables, and older central systems.

      02

      Truly DIY-installable

      Pre-charged MrCool DIY, Klimaire DIY, Perfect Aire ProDirect, Della, and Cooper & Hunter DIY lines install in a weekend. No refrigerant certification required.

      03

      Heats & cools in one unit

      Cold-climate models work below 0°F. Replace a furnace and AC with a single heat pump that runs year-round.

      04

      Zone your home

      Condition only the rooms you use. Multi-zone systems run up to five indoor heads off a single outdoor condenser.

      05

      Whisper-quiet operation

      Indoor heads run as low as 19 dB — quieter than a library. Outdoor units are a fraction of a central AC's noise floor.

      06

      $2,000 federal tax credit

      The Inflation Reduction Act's 25C credit covers 30% of qualifying heat pump costs, up to $2,000 per year — DIY installs included.

      § 04

      Questions before you buy

      Five quick answers
      What size mini split do I need for a 500 sq ft room?

      As a rough rule, 500 sq ft needs about 10,000–12,000 BTU (1 ton). But climate, insulation, and sun exposure can swing that ±25%. Use the calculator above for a real number.

      Can I really install a mini split myself?

      Yes — but only if you buy a pre-charged DIY model. Brands like MrCool DIY, Perfect Aire ProDirect, Klimaire DIY, Della DIY, and Cooper & Hunter DIY ship with refrigerant pre-loaded in the outdoor unit and Quick Connect flare fittings you hand-torque to spec. No EPA 608 certification, no vacuum pump, no refrigerant handling. Standard Mitsubishi / Daikin / Fujitsu / Pioneer / Senville lines are not pre-charged — those still require a licensed HVAC tech.

      How much does a mini split cost installed?

      Complete DIY single-zone (pre-charged): $1,200–$2,500 total, including the unit, lineset, and electrical. Pro-installed single-zone (standard brand): $3,500–$6,000. Pro multi-zone 3-head: $8,000–$15,000. You can deduct 30% (up to $2,000/year) via the federal 25C tax credit on qualifying models — that works for DIY installs too as long as the model is on the CEE list.

      Which mini split brand is the most reliable?

      There's no single "best" brand — it depends on whether you're installing it yourself or hiring a pro. For complete DIY: Klimaire DIY, MrCool, and Perfect Aire ProDirect all make solid pre-charged lines; Cooper & Hunter leads for cold-climate multi-zone; Della is the budget pick under $900. For pro-installed: Mitsubishi and Daikin lead on efficiency, cold-climate rating, and 10–12 year warranties. Full side-by-side breakdown in our buyer's guide.

      Do mini splits work in cold climates?

      Modern "hyper-heat" models (Mitsubishi H2i, Fujitsu XLTH, MrCool HyperHeat) maintain 100% rated capacity down to 5°F and still produce heat at -13°F. In zones 6–7 you still want a sized-up unit or backup heat strip.
