    FAQ

    Quick answers before you buy

    What size mini split do I need for a 500 sq ft room?

    As a rough rule, 500 sq ft needs about 10,000–12,000 BTU (1 ton). But climate, insulation, and sun exposure can swing that ±25%. Use the calculator above for a real number.

    Can I really install a mini split myself?

    Yes — if you buy a DIY-specific line like MrCool DIY or Perfect Aire DIY. These come with pre-charged refrigerant lines you just connect, so no EPA 608 certification is needed. Standard mini splits require a pro because you have to pull a vacuum and release refrigerant.

    How much does a mini split cost installed?

    DIY single-zone: $1,200–$2,500 total. Pro-installed single-zone: $3,500–$6,000. Multi-zone 3-head pro install: $8,000–$15,000. You can deduct 30% (up to $2,000/year) via the federal 25C tax credit on qualifying models.

    Which mini split brand is the most reliable?

    For reliability: Mitsubishi and Daikin lead (5–7 year warranties, best parts availability). For value: Pioneer and Senville offer 70% of the performance at half the price. For DIY: MrCool is the only major brand with a full pre-charged line. Full breakdown in our buyer's guide.

    Do mini splits work in cold climates?

    Modern "hyper-heat" models (Mitsubishi H2i, Fujitsu XLTH, MrCool HyperHeat) maintain 100% rated capacity down to 5°F and still produce heat at -13°F. In zones 6–7 you still want a sized-up unit or backup heat strip.
