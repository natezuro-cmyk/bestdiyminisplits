The DIY Mini Split Buying Checklist

01

Right-size it

  • Run our BTU calculator — don't trust sqft rules of thumb on Amazon
  • In climate zones 4 and colder, heating drives the size. Size on the heating load, not cooling
  • Kitchens: add +4,000 BTU for cooking load
  • Vaulted ceilings (12 ft+): multiply capacity by 1.4×
  • Poor insulation (1920s drafty): +20% vs average; spray-foamed new build: –15%
  • Never round up more than one standard size — oversizing short-cycles and kills the compressor
02

Cold-climate truth test

  • Marketing says "works in cold weather." Ignore it
  • Find the spec sheet. Look for rated capacity at 5°F (not just operating range)
  • If the spec-sheet 5°F number is missing or italicized, the unit will underperform below freezing
  • Only these DIY lines are truly rated cold-climate: MrCool HyperHeat, Cooper & Hunter Sophia
  • Pro-install cold-climate picks: Mitsubishi H2i, Fujitsu XLTH, Daikin Aurora
  • Your 99% design temp matters — find it at energystar.gov for your zip
03

Are you a good DIY candidate?

  • You're buying a pre-charged DIY model — not a standard Mitsubishi / Daikin line
  • You can run a 240V circuit, or will hire an electrician ($300–$600) for that one step
  • You have a helper for the afternoon (outdoor condenser = two-person lift)
  • The install is single-zone (or you're comfortable stepping up to multi-zone)
  • You own a torque wrench — or will buy one. Flare nuts MUST be torqued to spec
  • You're ok cutting a 3" hole through the exterior wall

05

7 spec-sheet questions to answer before you hit "Buy"

  • BTU capacity at 5°F (not just nominal) — the real cold-climate number
  • Minimum operating temperature — lower = better
  • SEER2 (cooling efficiency) — target 20+
  • HSPF2 (heating efficiency) — target 10+
  • Compressor warranty length on self-install (if DIY)
  • Indoor dB at low fan — under 30 dB is whisper-quiet
  • 25C / CEE certification — required for the $2k federal tax credit
