About Best DIY Mini Splits

An independent publication covering ductless mini splits for homeowners. Sizing tools, brand comparisons, and install playbooks — no sponsored content, no paid rankings, no fabricated reviews.

Who writes this

Best DIY Mini Splits is a small editorial project published under the site name rather than individual bylines. The people who build it prefer to focus on the work instead of the byline. What matters for readers is the process: research, cross-reference, write, edit, publish, update — applied consistently to every guide on the site. That process is spelled out on the methodology page.

Editorial principles

  • Homeowner-first. Every recommendation assumes you're putting one or two mini splits in a home you live in — not a 50-unit apartment complex. Advice is calibrated to that scale.
  • Spec-sheet over marketing. We quote numbers from AHRI filings and manufacturer submittal sheets. We don't repeat marketing copy like "works in cold weather" without verifying the rated capacity at 5°F.
  • Oversizing is a bigger enemy than undersizing. Most sales reps will push you toward a larger unit. Our sizing advice usually pulls in the opposite direction — because oversized mini splits short-cycle, fail to dehumidify, and die sooner.
  • DIY is legitimate. When the right answer is a pre-charged DIY unit you install yourself, we say so — even when that pick earns less affiliate commission than the alternative.
  • Transparency over polish. If we're unsure, we say so. If we're wrong, we correct it in place and note the correction.

How we work

  • Primary sources: AHRI Directory of Certified Product Performance, manufacturer submittal sheets, IRS Section 25C + CEE tier lists for tax-credit eligibility, DOE Energy Star Cold Climate database for cold-climate certification.
  • We read homeowner forums (Reddit r/HVAC, HVAC-Talk, Garage Journal) to catch reliability patterns that don't appear on spec sheets — premature compressor failure, board sensitivity, parts-availability lead times.
  • Every guide is reviewed at least twice a year — once before peak cooling season (late spring), once before peak heating season (late fall). Model years, efficiency ratings, and prices shift enough that stale guides become actively misleading.
  • When we make a factual error, we fix it and note the correction at the bottom of the article.

What we're not

  • We're not a manufacturer, contractor, or installer. We don't quote your install, diagnose your leak, or warranty a unit you bought elsewhere.
  • We don't run a thermal chamber. Real thermal testing requires an AHRI-certified facility that costs hundreds of thousands of dollars a year to operate. Any site claiming independent lab testing is almost certainly lying. We report AHRI-certified numbers.
  • We don't accept paid inclusion, paid rankings, sponsored placement, or "in exchange for coverage" arrangements. Affiliate links are disclosed; rankings aren't influenced by them.

Corrections and tips

Spot a factual error? Know a model we missed? See a new regulation or tax-credit change that should update a guide? Email hello@bestdiyminisplits.com. Every email is read; corrections happen fast.
