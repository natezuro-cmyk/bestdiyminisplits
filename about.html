The reason this site exists: the mini split buying process for a homeowner is a mess. Manufacturer marketing, affiliate farms, and half-informed YouTube channels dominate the search results. I wanted a resource that reads like field notes from someone who's actually done the work.

I test by reading every AHRI filing, every manufacturer spec sheet, and every homeowner complaint thread I can find — then I cross-reference. I don't accept paid placements. Our methodology page details how every recommendation is made.

What I believe about mini splits

  • Pre-charged DIY is legitimate. It's not a compromise — for many homeowners it's the right answer.
  • Oversizing is more common than undersizing. "Bigger is better" kills comfort and compressor life.
  • Cold climates deserve real cold-climate units — not branded "heat pump" units rated for 30°F minimum.
  • Transparency over polish. If I'm not sure, I say so. If I'm wrong, I correct it at the bottom of the page.

Not HVAC advice

This site is a publication, not a service provider. Nothing here substitutes for a local load calculation or licensed installer consultation. If your install involves panel upgrades, complex multi-zone layouts, or refrigerant handling, hire a pro — the buyer's guide explains when.
